Step-by-Step Harvesting Process

1

Identifying the Plant

During April and May, asafoetida plants emerge from the mountainous terrain, their roots containing the precious hing. Recognition is absolutely crucial at this stage, as farmers must spot the plant's distinct appearance to ensure successful harvesting before May's stems naturally fall.

Identifying Asafoetida

In spring, recognizing asafoetida amidst nature's diverse backdrop initiates the entire harvesting process. Experienced farmers can identify the characteristic leaves and growth patterns.

Mountain Terrain Navigation

The plant's image displays recognizable leaves atop mountainous regions. Farmers must navigate challenging terrain to locate these precious plants growing in their natural habitat.

2

Exposing Roots to Direct Sun

The asafoetida roots undergo 5â€“10 days of direct sun exposure before hing extraction begins. This vital step is crucial for proper resin preparation and ensures the development of the distinctive flavor profile that makes our hing exceptional.

Preservation via Sun Drying

Direct sunlight on asafoetida roots preserves the resin while enriching its flavorâ€”a traditional method that has been perfected over centuries for enhancing spice depth and potency.

Duration Balance

Careful balance in sun exposure duration is crucial. Too little exposure results in poor resin quality, while excessive exposure can negatively impact the taste and overall quality of the end product.

3

Extracting the Asafoetida

From mid-July to mid-September, skilled farmers perform sequential incisions on the root, patiently waiting for the precious resin to seep out before making additional cuts. This delicate method yields 30â€“50 kg of premium asafoetida from 3â€“4 thousand plants, with yields varying based on weather conditions.

Incising the Root

The extraction begins as experienced farmers skillfully make precise cuts on the plant's root, using traditional techniques passed down through generations to optimize resin collection.

Gathering Resin

Post-incisions, the precious asafoetida resin slowly emerges. Farmers meticulously gather every drop to minimize waste and maximize yield, ensuring no precious resin is lost in the process.

4

Storing and Packing for Export

By September-end, farmers carefully transport their precious produce down from the mountains to their homes. From there, it reaches our exporter's specialized storage facility, where it undergoes thorough quality checks and is professionally repacked in export-ready containers for shipping to India.

The Final Harvest

The asafoetida is carefully harvested and transported down from the hilly regions using traditional methods that preserve quality during the journey to processing facilities.

Quality Checks

Before exporting, every batch of asafoetida goes through our rigorous quality control processes to ensure it meets and exceeds the required standards for consumption and culinary use.

Export-Ready Packaging

The precious spice is professionally packaged using specialized materials and techniques that preserve its quality, potency, and aroma during transit, ensuring it reaches its destination in prime condition.
